The Concept

Sun, water,
and editorial.

This room heads outdoors — a private Pomona pool with arched bridges, golden-hour water, and hillside views built for summer editorial. One afternoon, styled for content, collaboration, and the kind of light you only get poolside.

This is not a meetup. It's a curated room of photographers, models, and a handful of brands and creative directors — chosen for taste, intention, and the energy that produces magazine-grade work in three hours.

Expect open water, summer styling, refreshments, and good vibes. Bring your best concepts. Leave with images that belong in your strongest portfolio.

Before You RSVP

Questions, answered.

What's included?

3 hours at a private poolside venue, access to all photographers and models in the room, refreshments and snacks, and the pre-event group chat with the full attendee list, concepts, and summer styling notes. Photographers/creators $90, models $50.

What should I bring?

Photographers: your camera, lighting if you prefer your own, a styled idea or two. Models: 2–3 summer/poolside looks (we'll share styling direction in advance), swimwear if you want in-water frames, and a towel. Brands: product, lookbook, anything you want photographed.

What's the dress code?

Poolside editorial. Think summer — swimwear, linen, bright color, anything that reads well on water and in sun. We share a moodboard with the full theme in the group chat a week prior.

Where's the exact location?

It's a private estate pool in Pomona. The exact address, parking instructions, and arrival timing are released to confirmed attendees 48 hours before the event.

How do I RSVP and pay?

RSVP through our Partiful page — it handles your spot and payment. Photographers/creators $90, models $50. Spots are first come, and the room is capped at 35.

How do I get final images?

Photographers retain their files. Most share previews within 48 hours and edited galleries within 2 weeks via the event group chat. Models can DM photographers directly to license/use specific frames.
