The Field Guide

How to Prepare
for a Create &
Connect Editorial
Event.

What to bring, how to show up, and how to walk away with portfolio-worthy work — and the right people in your corner.

A curated guide for models, photographers & creatives · Los Angeles
First, the mindset

You're not attending
a meetup. You're building work.

The difference between a forgettable afternoon and a portfolio you're proud of is decided before you ever arrive.

Every Create & Connect room is kept intentionally small and intentionally curated — a balanced mix of models, photographers, and the occasional brand or stylist, chosen so the work that comes out of it actually elevates everyone in it. That curation only pays off if you treat the day like a shoot, not a social.

Come in with a point of view. Know the look you want to walk away with, who you'd love to create it with, and what you'd be proud to add to your portfolio. The most memorable creatives in our rooms aren't the loudest — they're the most intentional.

The goal isn't to shoot with everyone. It's to make two or three images you'd actually put at the top of your portfolio — and to meet the people you'll keep creating with long after the event ends.

Before you arrive, get clear on three things

  • Your intention. One editorial look, one mood, one story you want to tell.
  • Your collaborators. Scan the room early; identify who you'd love to create with.
  • Your takeaway. The two or three frames that would earn a place in your book.
Curate your look

Styling is the story.

Editorial work lives or dies on intention. Every piece you bring should earn its place.

We always share the theme, venue, and a moodboard ahead of time — read it closely. The strongest looks feel considered against the space and the concept, never thrown together. When in doubt, choose fewer pieces, better chosen.

Build your wardrobe around the concept

  • Bring options, not a costume. Two or three intentional looks beat one over-styled outfit.
  • Mind the palette. Tonal, textural, and earthy reads more editorial than loud logos or busy prints.
  • Think in textures. Linen, silk, leather, knit — texture photographs as richness.
  • Accessories finish a frame. Jewelry, a hat, sunglasses, a single statement piece.
  • Steam everything. Wrinkles are the fastest way to make elevated styling look careless.

Hair, beauty & the details

  • Arrive camera-ready unless a MUA or stylist is confirmed for the event.
  • Bring touch-up essentials: blotting paper, lip, a compact, hair ties, pins, a brush.
  • Skin first. Hydrated, well-prepped skin photographs better than heavy product.
A good rule: if a stylist at a magazine would pull it for the concept, bring it. If it only works because it's your favorite, leave it home.
For models

Presence is a skill.

The camera rewards preparation. Know a handful of poses and expressions that work for your body and your face before you arrive — then let the direction take you somewhere new.

  • Warm up beforehand. Run angles in the mirror so movement feels natural, not stiff.
  • Bring references. A small set of saved editorial images keeps you and the photographer aligned.
  • Move slowly. Small, deliberate adjustments give photographers more usable frames.
  • Trust the room. Take direction, offer ideas, and treat every photographer as a collaborator.
  • Pack the kit. Nude undergarments, backup shoes, a robe, water, and snacks for energy.
For photographers

Show up with a vision.

The photographers who leave our events with standout work arrive with a plan — a look, a light, and a shot list — and stay flexible enough to chase the moment when it appears.

  • Charge and clear. Batteries full, cards formatted, a backup card in the bag.
  • Pre-visualize. Build a loose shot list and a moodboard tied to the theme.
  • Pack light you can control. A reflector or a small portable light expands what's possible indoors.
  • Scout fast. Walk the venue early and claim the light before the room fills.
  • Direct with warmth. Clear, kind direction gets better frames than silence behind the lens.
The day of

Respect the room
and the time.

  • Arrive on time — or early. The first light and the calmest setups belong to those who show up ready.
  • Introduce yourself. A name, an Instagram, and what you're hoping to create opens every door.
  • Share, don't claim. Rotate through collaborators; the room works when everyone leaves with work.
  • Protect the space. Respect the venue, the host, and the people — curation is a privilege, not a guarantee.
  • Stay present. Phones down between sets. The best collaborations start in conversation.
After the event

The work doesn't end
when the shoot does.

  • Exchange before you leave. Confirm handles so images find their way back to everyone involved.
  • Deliver with intention. Photographers: send selects promptly. Models: give edits time and care.
  • Credit everyone. Tag the photographer, the models, the stylist, the brands, and Create & Connect.
  • Curate what you post. Your feed is your portfolio — share the frames that read as your best taste.
  • Keep the connection. The people you met are the start of your next shoot, not just this one.
Portfolio-worthy work is the byproduct. The real return is a roster of collaborators who operate at your level — and call you first next time.
You're ready

Now come build
something worth keeping.

One curated event a month. Every venue, every theme, a new editorial world — and a room full of people who take the work as seriously as you do.
